Spatchcock Roast Turkey

Adapted from NYT Cooking

Serves: 8
Active: 30 min
Cook: 70-80 min
Rest: 15 min

Ingredients

  • 12-14 lb whole turkey
  • 6 tbsp Diamond Crystal kosher salt (or 4 tbsp Morton's)
  • 2-3 tbsp olive oil
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Fresh thyme, sage, rosemary sprigs (optional garnish)

Instructions

  1. Spatchcock (Tuesday): Place turkey breast-down. Cut along both sides of backbone with kitchen shears. Remove backbone. Flip turkey, press firmly on breastbone until it cracks and bird lies flat.
  2. Dry brine (Tuesday): Pat completely dry. Loosen skin over breasts, season underneath with 2 tsp salt. Season entire surface generously with remaining salt. Place on baking sheet, refrigerate uncovered 12-48 hours.
  3. Bring to room temp (Thursday 10 AM): Remove from fridge 30-60 minutes before cooking.
  4. Prep & roast (Thursday 11 AM): Preheat oven to 425¬∞F. Brush turkey with olive oil, season with pepper. Roast 30 minutes at 425¬∞F.
  5. Reduce heat: Lower oven to 350¬∞F. Continue roasting 40-50 minutes until breast reaches 155¬∞F (thigh 175¬∞F).
  6. Rest (12:30 PM): Remove when breast hits 155¬∞F. Tent loosely with foil, rest 15 minutes. Temperature will rise to 165¬∞F.
Key Tips

‚Ä¢ Use an instant-read thermometer - it's the only way to know it's done
‚Ä¢ The backbone makes excellent stock - roast it with onion, carrot, celery
‚Ä¢ Carve the turkey on a cutting board with a channel to catch juices

Herby Mushroom Croissant Stuffing

Half Baked Harvest

Serves: 8-10
Active: 30 min
Bake: 45-50 min

Ingredients

  • 12 cups torn croissants (6-8 croissants)
  • 8 tbsp (1 stick) butter, divided
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 3 celery stalks, chopped
  • 3-4 cups mixed mushrooms, sliced
  • Salt and pepper
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups Tuscan kale, torn
  • 2 tbsp fresh sage, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme, chopped
  • 2-3 cups vegetable broth
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 cup shredded Gruyere

Instructions

  1. Toast croissants (Wed): Preheat oven to 350¬∞F. Grease 9x13 dish. Spread torn croissants in dish, dot with 2 tbsp sliced butter. Bake 5-8 minutes until lightly toasted. Store in bag.
  2. Saut√© vegetables (Wed): Heat oil over medium. Cook onion 3-5 minutes. Add celery and mushrooms, season with salt/pepper. Cook undisturbed 5 minutes, then stir and cook 3-5 more minutes.
  3. Add aromatics (Wed): Add remaining 6 tbsp butter, garlic, kale, sage, thyme. Cook until kale wilts, about 5 minutes. Cool and refrigerate.
  4. Assemble (Thu 9 AM): Whisk 2 cups broth with eggs. Combine with croissants and vegetable mixture. Toss gently. Add more broth if dry. Pour into greased dish, top with Gruyere. Cover with foil.
  5. Bake (Thu 12:45 PM): Bake covered at 350¬∞F for 35 minutes. Remove foil, bake 10-15 more minutes until golden.

Roasted Root Vegetables with Crispy Sage

Love and Lemons

Serves: 8
Prep: 20 min
Roast: 25-50 min

Ingredients

  • 1-1/2 lbs carrots, peeled, cut into 1" chunks
  • 1-1/2 lbs parsnips, peeled, cut into 1" chunks
  • 1 lb sweet potato, cut into 1" chunks
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh sage, chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • Salt and pepper
  • Crispy Sage:
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 10 fresh sage leaves

Instructions

  1. Prep vegetables (Wed): Peel and chop all vegetables. Store in water in fridge.
  2. Roast (Thu 1:30 PM): Preheat oven to 425¬∞F. Line 2 baking sheets with parchment. Drain vegetables well, pat dry.
  3. Season: Toss vegetables with 1/4 cup oil, herbs, salt, pepper. Spread in single layers on sheets.
  4. Roast: Roast 25-50 minutes, rotating pans halfway. Parsnips and sweet potatoes finish first (25-35 min). Carrots take longer (40-50 min).
  5. Crispy sage: Heat 2 tbsp oil in small pan until bubbling. Add sage leaves, cook 1 minute until crisp. Transfer to paper towels. Reserve oil.
  6. Serve: Transfer vegetables to platter. Drizzle with 1 tbsp sage oil, top with crispy sage.

Martha Stewart Mac & Cheese

Martha Stewart

Serves: 8-10
Active: 30 min
Bake: 40-45 min

Ingredients

  • 8 tbsp (1 stick) butter, plus 2 tbsp
  • 6 slices white bread, torn into 1/4" pieces
  • 5-1/2 cups whole milk
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne
  • 4-1/2 cups (18 oz) sharp white cheddar, grated
  • 2 cups (8 oz) Gruyere, grated
  • 1 lb elbow macaroni

Instructions

  1. Prep (Thu 8 AM): Preheat oven to 375¬∞F. Butter a 9x13" dish. Toss bread with 2 tbsp melted butter.
  2. Heat milk: Warm milk in saucepan over medium (don't boil).
  3. Make roux: Melt 6 tbsp butter in large pot. Add flour, whisk 1 minute.
  4. Make sauce: Slowly whisk in hot milk. Cook, whisking constantly, until thick and bubbly.
  5. Add cheese: Remove from heat. Stir in salt, spices, 3 cups cheddar, 1-1/2 cups Gruyere until melted.
  6. Cook pasta: Boil macaroni 2-3 minutes LESS than package says. Drain, rinse with cold water.
  7. Combine: Stir pasta into cheese sauce. Pour into prepared dish.
  8. Top: Sprinkle remaining 1-1/2 cups cheddar and 1/2 cup Gruyere. Top with breadcrumbs. Cover with foil.
  9. Bake (Thu 1:30 PM): Bake covered 30 minutes. Uncover, bake 10-15 more until golden. Broil briefly if needed.

Make-Ahead Roasted Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Half Baked Harvest

Serves: 8-10
Active: 30 min
Cook: 25 min + 45 for garlic

Ingredients

  • 1 head garlic
  • Olive oil
  • 5 lbs Yukon gold potatoes, peeled, cubed
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 3-4 sprigs fresh sage
  • 3-4 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 8 tbsp (1 stick) butter
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional finish:
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • Fresh sage for brown butter

Instructions

  1. Roast garlic (Wed): Preheat oven to 400¬∞F. Slice top off garlic, drizzle with oil, wrap in foil. Roast 45-55 minutes until golden and soft.
  2. Cook potatoes (Wed): In Dutch oven, combine potatoes, cream, milk, sage and thyme sprigs. Bring to gentle boil, then simmer 20-25 minutes until fork-tender.
  3. Drain: Drain potatoes, RESERVING all the cream. Discard herb sprigs.
  4. Mash: Press potatoes and squeezed garlic cloves through potato ricer into warm pot (or mash well).
  5. Finish: Over low heat, stir in 1-1/2 cups reserved cream and butter. Add more cream to reach desired consistency. Season generously with salt and pepper.
  6. Store: Transfer to 9x13" baking dish. Cover and refrigerate up to 3 days.
  7. Reheat (Thu 12:45 PM): Pour 1/2 cup milk/cream and 2 tbsp butter over cold potatoes (don't stir). Cover with foil. Bake at 350¬∞F for 25-30 minutes. Stir before serving.

Lemon Garlic Green Beans

Classic

Serves: 8
Total: 10 min

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs green beans, trimmed
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Juice of 1-2 lemons
  • Salt and pepper
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prep (Wed): Trim green beans. Store in bag in fridge. Mince garlic, store separately.
  2. Blanch (Thu 2:15 PM): Bring large pot of salted water to boil. Add beans, cook 4-5 minutes until tender-crisp and bright green. Drain well.
  3. Make dressing: In large bowl, whisk oil, garlic, lemon zest, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  4. Toss: Add warm beans, toss to coat. Taste, adjust seasoning. Add red pepper flakes if desired.
  5. Serve: Transfer to dish and serve immediately.

Mushroom Gravy

Love and Lemons

Serves: 8
Makes: ~3 cups
Total: 45 min

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped (1/3 cup)
  • 1 lb cremini mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 tbsp tamari or soy sauce
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-1/2 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 1/2 tbsp fresh rosemary, chopped
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions

  1. Make (Tuesday): Heat oil in large skillet over medium. Add shallot, cook until soft, about 4 minutes.
  2. Cook mushrooms: Add mushrooms, cook until soft and releasing liquid, 8-10 minutes.
  3. Add aromatics: Stir in tamari, garlic, thyme, rosemary. Sprinkle flour over mushrooms, stir 1 minute.
  4. Add broth: Pour in broth, bring to simmer. Cook, whisking often, until thickened, about 20 minutes. Should coat back of spoon.
  5. Season and store: Season with salt and pepper. Cool completely, store in airtight container in fridge up to 3 days.
  6. Reheat (Thu 2:30 PM): Warm over low heat, stirring in a splash of broth or water if too thick. Taste and adjust seasoning.
Gluten-Free Option

Skip flour in step 3. After adding broth, mix 2 tbsp cornstarch with a few tbsp hot gravy until smooth. Stir back into pan and cook until thickened.

Cinnamon Apple Crumble Cake

Half Baked Harvest

Serves: 10-12
Active: 30 min
Bake: 50-60 min

Ingredients

  • Cake:
  • 1/2 cup + 1 tbsp butter, room temp
  • 2/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 eggs
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 4-5 baking apples, cored and sliced
  • Crumble:
  • 1-1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2/3 cup + 2 tsp sugar
  • 2-1/8 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup + 1 tbsp melted butter
  • To serve:
  • Powdered sugar
  • Vanilla ice cream

Instructions

  1. Prep (Tuesday): Preheat oven to 350¬∞F. Butter a 10" springform pan.
  2. Make batter: Beat butter, sugar, and vanilla until light and fluffy. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each.
  3. Add dry: Whisk flour, baking powder, salt. Add to butter mixture, mix until just combined.
  4. Assemble: Spread batter in pan. Arrange apple slices on top, pressing gently into batter.
  5. Make crumble: Whisk flour, 2/3 cup sugar, 2 tsp cinnamon. Stir in vanilla and melted butter until crumbly. Sprinkle over apples.
  6. Final topping: Mix 2 tsp sugar with 1/8 tsp cinnamon, sprinkle over crumble.
  7. Bake: Bake 50-60 minutes until golden. Skewer should come out with just a few moist crumbs.
  8. Cool and store: Cool 15 minutes in pan. Remove sides. Cool completely. Cover tightly, store at room temp up to 2 days.
  9. Serve (Thu): Dust with powdered sugar. Warm individual slices in microwave 20-30 seconds. Serve with ice cream.
