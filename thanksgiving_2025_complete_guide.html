ü¶É Spatchcock Roast Turkey

NYT Cooking / Various sources

The star of the show! Spatchcocking (removing the backbone) allows the turkey to cook evenly and quickly - about 70-80 minutes total. You'll get perfectly crispy skin and juicy meat every time.

Serves: 8 people
Cook Time: 70-80 minutes
Prep Ahead: Dry brine 2-3 days before

Ingredients

  • 12-14 lb whole turkey
  • 6 tablespoons Diamond Crystal kosher salt (or 4 tbsp Morton's)
  • 2-3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Fresh black pepper
  • Optional: fresh thyme, sage, and rosemary sprigs

Instructions

  1. Spatchcock the turkey (2-3 days before): Place turkey breast-side down. Using sharp kitchen shears, cut along both sides of the backbone and remove it (save for stock). Flip turkey over and press down firmly on the breastbone to flatten.
  2. Dry brine: Pat turkey completely dry with paper towels. Carefully loosen skin from breasts. Season each breast with 1 teaspoon salt under the skin. Sprinkle remaining salt evenly over entire turkey. Place on a baking sheet, refrigerate uncovered for 12-48 hours.
  3. Thanksgiving Day: Remove turkey from fridge 30-60 minutes before cooking. Preheat oven to 425¬∞F.
  4. Prepare for roasting: Brush turkey lightly with olive oil, season with black pepper. Place on a rimmed baking sheet or roasting pan.
  5. Roast: Place turkey in center of oven. Roast at 425¬∞F for 30 minutes. Reduce heat to 350¬∞F and continue roasting for 40-50 minutes, until thickest part of breast reaches 155¬∞F (carryover heat will bring it to 165¬∞F).
  6. Rest: Remove turkey from oven, tent loosely with foil. Let rest 15 minutes before carving. Temperature will rise to 165¬∞F during rest.
üí° Pro Tips:

‚Ä¢ The backbone can be roasted separately with vegetables for amazing gravy
‚Ä¢ Use an instant-read thermometer for accuracy
‚Ä¢ Don't skip the resting time - it allows juices to redistribute

Martha Stewart / Food52

The gold standard of baked mac and cheese. Sharp white cheddar and Gruy√®re create a creamy, sophisticated sauce, topped with golden buttered breadcrumbs.

Serves: 8-10
Cook Time: 30 minutes
Prep Ahead: Assemble day-of

Ingredients

  • 8 tablespoons (1 stick) butter, plus 2 tbsp for breadcrumbs
  • 6 slices white bread, crusts removed, torn into ¬º-¬Ω inch pieces
  • 5¬Ω cups milk
  • ¬Ω cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons salt, plus more for pasta water
  • ¬º teaspoon nutmeg
  • ¬º teaspoon black pepper

Instructions

  1. Prep: Preheat oven to 375¬∞F. Butter a 3-quart (9x13 inch) casserole dish.

  5. Make b√©chamel: Slowly pour hot milk into flour-butter mixture while whisking constantly. Continue cooking, whisking constantly, until mixture bubbles and thickens considerably.

  7. Cook pasta: Bring large pot of salted water to boil. Cook macaroni 2-3 minutes LESS than package directions (pasta should still have a bite). Drain, rinse under cold water, drain well.
  8. Combine: Stir macaroni into cheese sauce until well coated.
  9. Assemble: Pour mixture into prepared dish. Sprinkle remaining 1¬Ω cups cheddar and ¬Ω cup Gruy√®re over top. Scatter breadcrumbs over cheese.
  10. Bake: Cover with foil, bake 30 minutes. Uncover and bake 10-15 more minutes until golden brown on top. If not brown enough, broil briefly (watch carefully!). Let rest 5 minutes before serving.

Undercooking the pasta is key - it finishes cooking in the oven and absorbs the sauce perfectly. The combination of sharp cheddar (for flavor) and Gruy√®re (for smoothness) is magic!

ü•î Make-Ahead Roasted Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Half Baked Harvest

The creamiest, most flavorful mashed potatoes you'll ever make. Cooking the potatoes in milk and cream infuses them with richness, while roasted garlic adds sweet, mellow flavor.

Serves: 8-10
Cook Time: 25 minutes + 45 for garlic
Prep Ahead: Yes! Make 1-2 days before

Ingredients

  • 1 head garlic
  • Olive oil
  • 4-5 lbs Yukon gold potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 3-4 sprigs fresh sage
  • 3-4 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 8 tablespoons (1 stick) butter
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional for serving:
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Fresh sage or thyme for brown butter swirl

Instructions

  1. Roast garlic: Preheat oven to 400¬∞F. Slice off top portion of garlic head to expose cloves. Place on foil, drizzle with olive oil, wrap up. Roast 40-55 minutes until deeply golden and very soft. Let cool, then squeeze out cloves.
  2. Cook potatoes: In a large Dutch oven, combine potatoes, heavy cream, milk, sage sprigs, and thyme sprigs. Bring to a gentle boil over medium heat. Reduce heat and cook 20-25 minutes until potatoes are fork-tender.
  3. Drain and save cream: Drain potatoes, RESERVING all the cream mixture. Discard herb sprigs.
  4. Mash: Press potatoes and roasted garlic cloves through a potato ricer back into the warm pot. (Or mash with a potato masher in the pot.)
  5. Add cream and butter: Over low heat, add 1¬Ω cups of the reserved cream and the stick of butter. Stir gently. Continue adding cream until you reach desired consistency. Season generously with salt and pepper.
  6. Optional finishing touch: Brown 2 tablespoons butter with fresh sage or thyme in a small pan. Swirl into potatoes just before serving.
  7. Serve: Transfer to serving bowl and serve warm and creamy!
üí° Make-Ahead Instructions:

In Oven: Make through step 5. Transfer to baking dish, cover, refrigerate up to 3 days. To reheat: Preheat oven to 325¬∞F. Pour ¬Ω cup milk/cream over potatoes + 2 tbsp butter (don't stir). Cover with foil, bake 20-25 minutes until warmed. Stir before serving.

In Slow Cooker: Transfer finished potatoes to slow cooker, keep on low up to 4 hours. 30 minutes before serving, add ¬Ω cup cream + 2 tbsp butter on top (don't stir). Let warm through, then stir.

ü´ò Lemon Garlic Green Beans

Simple & Fresh

Fresh, bright, and simple - these green beans provide a light, crisp contrast to the richer dishes. The lemon and garlic keep them from being boring!

Serves: 8
Cook Time: 10 minutes
Prep Ahead: Trim day before

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs fresh green beans, trimmed
  • 2-3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Juice of 1-2 lemons
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Optional: red pepper flakes

Instructions

  3. Make dressing: In a large bowl, whisk together olive oil, minced garlic, lemon zest, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  4. Toss: Add warm green beans to the bowl and toss well to coat.
  5. Adjust: Taste and add more lemon juice, salt, or pepper as needed. Add a pinch of red pepper flakes if you like a little heat.
  6. Serve: Transfer to serving dish and serve immediately while warm.
üí° Timing Tip:

Make these while other dishes are finishing in the oven. They come together in 10 minutes and are best served immediately. You can blanch them ahead of time and store in fridge, then just toss with the warm dressing before serving.

üçÑ Vegan Mushroom Gravy

Love and Lemons

Rich, velvety, and packed with umami flavor. This vegan gravy is so good, even meat lovers won't miss traditional turkey gravy. Perfect make-ahead dish!

Serves: 8 (makes ~3 cups)
Cook Time: 35 minutes
Prep Ahead: Make 2-3 days before

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped (‚Öì cup)
  • 1 lb cremini mushrooms, sliced
  • ¬Ω tablespoon tamari or soy sauce
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1¬Ω tablespoons fresh thyme leaves
  • ¬Ω tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped
  • ¬º cup all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups vegetable broth
  • Sea salt and black pepper

Instructions

  1. Saut√© shallot: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add shallot and cook until soft, about 4 minutes.
  2. Cook mushrooms: Add mushrooms and cook until soft and releasing their liquid, about 8-10 minutes. Stir in tamari, garlic, thyme, and rosemary.
  3. Add flour: Sprinkle flour over the mushrooms and stir for 1 minute to cook out the raw flour taste.
  4. Add broth: Pour in vegetable broth and bring to a simmer. Simmer until thickened, whisking often, about 20 minutes. The gravy should coat the back of a spoon.
  5. Season: Taste and season generously with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve or store: Serve warm over mashed potatoes, turkey, or stuffing!
üí° Make-Ahead & Reheating:

This gravy is perfect to make 2-3 days ahead! Store in an airtight container in the fridge. It will thicken as it sits. To reheat: Warm on stovetop over low heat, stirring in a splash of vegetable broth or water until you reach desired consistency. Taste and adjust seasoning.

Gluten-Free Option: Skip the flour in step 3. After adding broth, mix 2 tablespoons cornstarch with a few tablespoons of the hot gravy in a small bowl until smooth. Pour this mixture back into the pan and stir until gravy thickens.
